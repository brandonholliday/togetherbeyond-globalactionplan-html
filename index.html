TogetherBeyond is our approach to living inclusion every day and enabling diversity and equality globally. For us, this means creating a culture of belonging where we all thrive and embracing all perspectives.

What is the Global Action Plan?

The Global Action Plan for Advancing Justice & Equality was released in 2020 in response to the brutal deaths of George Floyd, Breonna Taylor, Ahmaud Arbery and many other black men, women and children. These events shone a light on injustices in our society and pushed us to reflect on what else we could be doing as a company to drive change. The aim of the Action Plan was to achieve equality for all employees, not just black employees. This is shown through our efforts to recruit, retain and develop a range of diverse employees, including our female, Latinx, Asian, LGBTQ+, Veteran and physically diverse colleagues.

What have we achieved so far?

Since the birth of the Action Plan, we are on track to invest over 10 million dollars by the summer of 2025. Our targeted charitable donations, charterships and STEAM outreach efforts that directly impact historically underrepresented groups across the world. We are also supporting some flagship program such as the SEED school (a boarding school for local students located in a historically deprived area of South Los Angeles).

We are also working towards developing our company to better reflects the communities we serve. Five of our nine board members are diverse leaders and our executive leadership team is over fifty percent female. In addition, our executives serve as executive sponsors to our Jacobs Employee Networks. Last year we introduced measures to make sure that all leaders, Vice President level and above mentor an employee of different identity, ethnicity, background or ability to themselves and we now have over 500 employees in the mentorship program.

In 2020 and 2021 all of our employees completed Unconscious Bias and Advocacy & Alley training - fostering an environment that helps understand how to be more inclusive leaders, colleagues and community members. We have also sent over 400 employees from Black, Latino or Asian backgrounds through a management accelerator program. And we have increased the diversity of our talent pool by partnering with industry leading organizations such as the National Society of Black Engineers, Black Engineer of the Year, the society of Hispanic Professional Engineers, the Society of Women Engineers and Building Equality, a leading LGBTQ alliance in the UK. Check out this dashboard to see achievements across the regions!

What now?

We will continue to move our Action Plan forward and our progress will be tracked quarterly in Global Action Plan Dashboard. Exciting global work ahead to note includes:

  1. Our work with the Frontier Program as Jacobs supports students at the Drake Stake Community College, a HBCU partnered with NASA for the Moon to Mars Program. For 2023-2025 Jacobs has already committment to this program includes equipment and scholarships, throughout the four-year program.
  2. Through the SEED LA School Foundation, Jacobs is funding the construction and opening of the SEED LA school every single year. You can find out more on this by watching the Harambee event – BHM Planting a SEED for Equity and Access 2022-2024.

Global TogetherBeyond Strategy: Strategic Pillars

TogetherBeyond is our approach to living inclusion every day and enabling diversity and equality globally. For us, this means creating a culture of belonging where we all thrive and embracing all perspectives.

Strategic Pillar

Culture Building
& Engagement Building

Building awareness and engagement around I&D promotes our culture of caring to support our efforts to retain great talent.

Strategic Pillar

Leadership Commitment
& Accountability

An inclusive culture requires commitment and accountability from not only top leadership but every employee.

Strategic Pillar

Developing our
Talent Building

Building an I&D culture provides a foundation for all our talent centered initiatives so that Jacobs attracts and retains the world's best.

Strategic Pillar

Growing our
Business

Our widely different perspectives and talents greatly enhance our ability to provide clients with more imaginative and effective solutions.

The Action Plan aligns with these strategic pillars and has three central focus areas

Amplify Culture of Belonging

Expand beyond our Conscious Inclusion program, training the full 55,000-person global workforce in Bystander Intervention by the end of FY21.

Recruit, retain, and advance Black employees based on merit

Increase representation of Black employees at all levels over the next three years to proportionally reflect the overall external population, and provide continuous professional development and advancement. Partner with external organizations to provide leadership development programs to accelerate advancement for Black employees to mid- and senior-level leadership.

Contribute to Structural Change in Broader Society

Donate $10M over the next five years in support of Black educational and professional development.
